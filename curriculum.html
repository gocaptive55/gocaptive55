Curriculum Overview

2 cycles (3 months each), 4 modules per cycle

Our curriculum consists of 8 modules total; each module is designed to take place after school or in the early evening, with flexible class-times. Clients may take a few as 2 or as many as 4 modules per cycle, depending on their needs. A special component to our program is the opportunity to mentor. Introducing even our youngest clients to the peer mentoring model in the first cycle has benefits such as increased responsibility and the development of self-efficacy in self-advocacy.

A client completes 2-4 modules in the first 3-month-cycle: 
Art Therapy 1
Economic Empowerment 1
Family/Social Support Services
Peer Support/Mentoring 1

Then he or she moves onto the second 3-month-cycle:
Art Therapy 2
Economic Empowerment 2
Family Maintenance Skills
Peer Support/Mentoring 2

Modules - In depth

Our modules are unique in that the build upon eachother when taken in sequence. Our clients rave about their opportunity to mentor others because it helps build trust, new relationships, improve communication and highlights many important aspects of what one has to offer. Many of our clients experience long-term friendships with their mentees and go on to volunteer in other capacities in local organations. Mentoring provides an opportunity to give back and tap into individual strengths, building self-esteem and promoting a positivity and well-being.

Art Therapy 1

module overview

Art Therapy 1 serves as a self-care module that helps the client explore deeper issues and reinforce positive coping mechanisms. There will opportunities to create a new sense of belonging among positive peers and learn strategies to reduce interactions with negative influences. We will look at the family structure, and throught the therapeutic process, identify roles and power structures that may contribute to low-self-worth. We will use art to mediate negative impulses and focus on positive visualization through the media.

Goals for sessions

Increasing Self-worth/Self-image
- Establish positive self-image and feelings of self-worth separate from affiliating with negative peer groups
- Create a sense of belonging withing positive peer relationships
- Practice engaging in socially appropriate behaviors
- Psychoeducation on behaviors, emotions and attitudes and repercussions for negative choices

Social Skills
- Develop social skills necessary to maintain positive relationships
- Identify and verbalize needs met through negative peer groups
- Looking at the family: what are the rules?
- Reward systems for participating in desired social behaviors

Negative to positive
- Looking at negative statements/ideas/feelings and transitioning to positive
- Implementing effective coping strategies
- Mediation of feelings using art

outcome measures

We track our participants progress using simple but informative questionnaires at the beginning, at a mid-point and at the conclusion of each program. Informational interviewing will also be performed to round out our 5-point Likert-scale data gathering.

Example questions:
On a scale of 1-5;
1 being Not prepared at all
2 being Not prepared but working on it
3 being Neither prepared or not prepared
4 being I feel somewhat prepared
5 being I feel very prepared

"On a scale of 1-5 how prepared are you engage in positive social relationships?"
"On a scale of 1-5 how prepared are you practice socially appropriate behaviors?"
"On a scale of 1-5 how prepared are you use effective coping strategies when you feel overwhelmed?"

Family/Social Support Services

module overview

Introduction to support groups provides introductions to organizations such as Alcoholics Anonymous, SAMSHA, SMART Recovery, Alanon and Alateen. Volunteers from these organizations will present on the benefits of utilizing these free/low cost resources. Individual support modalities will be covered in depth, providing clients with additional modes of self-care.

- SMART Recovery
- Narcotics Anonymous

Introduction to individual support modalities

- Art Therapy
- Acupuncture

Introduction to self-care practices

- Journaling

Art Therapy 2

module overview

Art Therapy 2 serves as a self-care module that helps the client explore ongoing self-care including exploration into the importance of attending and regularly participating in group sessions to develop positive social skills, replacing negative peer influences, expressing rejection through the art process, establishing a social skills support group and releasing rigidity using a somatic approch.

Goals for sessions

Ongoing self-care 2
- Importance of attending and regularly participating in group sessions to develop positive social skills
- How do I continue to meet my needs outside of negative peer influences
- Experimenting with media that engages me fully
- Expression of past rejection experiences through art

Social Skills 2
- Identifying positive skills to improve friendships
- Increase involvement in positive social activities
- Increase frequency of peer-involvement
- Who can I turn to for support?

Negative to positive 2
- Replacing low-self esteem dialog with secure self-talk and artful expression
- Releasing rigidity somatically, through the art process
- Exchanging poor role models with positive role models, interviewing and visionboarding
