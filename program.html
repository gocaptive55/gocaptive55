GOALS

This program has been designed to help successfully integrate/re-integrate clients into important aspects of life after incarceration. We focus on Integration and reintegration into family, work, education, with a special self-care component. Our program is structured to reinforce important building blocks which may help reduce the rate of recidivism.

Clients will participate in various groups and courses, such as education and parenting, support for those with a history of substance abuse, time management, and education on current offerings for public assistance.

Who we serve in support of our mission... 

Incarcerated youth have many barriers from many different areas standing before them as they prepare to enter (or re-enter) the workforce and transition back to daily life. For example, heroin use among incarcerated youth is related to poor school attendance, younger onset of drug use, peers who also use illicit drugs, polydrug use, and child abuse (Chiang, Chen, Sun, Chan, & Chen, 2006). In addition to drug use, research has shown that individuals who become involved with the justice system at a young age can be more susceptible to poor life outcomes (O’Niell, Strnadova, & Cummings, 2017).

The same research has also shown that educational deficits can also occur, which in turn will impact future employment options, and eventually long term earnings and employability. Additionally, families can pose barriers for these individuals as well in the form of lacking structure, food, stability, and safe shelter (O’Niell et al., 2017). Other factors that play a role in the success of these individuals include their neighborhood, their community, how they now perceived as an individual, and social climate.

Altschuler & Brash (2004) have conducted research demonstrating that it is critical to intervene with these individuals as soon as possible during their transition period. The transition period in which these youths re-enter their typical lives is viewed anywhere from two months pre-release to six months post-release. Research has shown that the time after release is when interventions should occur, meaning that our workshops should be put into place during those times.

Our services will be helpful at other times in their lives, but they will produce the best outcomes if implemented during this critical period. We believe that the workshops and seminars offered at our facility can greatly improve the quality of life for incarcerated youth. The programs we offer will benefit these individuals by directly combating the barriers that stand in their way during their transition. 

Altschuler, D. M., & Brash, R. (2004). Adolescent and teenage offenders confronting the challenges and opportunities of reentry. Youth Violence and Juvenile Justice, 2(1), 72-87.

Chiang, S. C., Chen, S. J., Sun, H. J., Chan, H. Y., & Chen, W. J. (2006). Heroin use among youths incarcerated for illicit drug use: psychosocial environment, substance use history, psychiatric comorbidity, and route of administration. American journal on addictions, 15(3), 233-241.

O'Neill, S. C., Strnadová, I., & Cumming, T. M. (2017). Systems barriers to community re-entry for incarcerated youths: A review. Children and Youth Services Review, 79(C), 29-36.

ANNALISE MAKE THIS HAPPEN WOMAN

Our programming will address the needs of our clients by working towards the following goals:

Better family relationships

-Bring in speakers who have had family members incarcerated to show different points of view
-Send home “homework” that includes other family members
-Host family events
-provide education on alcohol and substance use and its effects on family units
-Groups on healthy and unhealthy relationships and communication 

Help clients find work

-Job preparation;resume building, career assessments
-Host job fairs and provide transportation to local job fairs/ hiring events
-discuss time management skills and create an example time table for a clients day/week

Home-making skills

-Teach basics of finances and tax paying/balancing a checkbook
-Training on how to do laundry, grocery shopping etc
-help clients create a custom personalized budget for appropriate monthly spending
-increase awareness into availability of benefits and assist clients in applying for and keeping government benefits

Increase educational opportunities

-Connect clients with GED courses
-Work with local trade schools or BOCES to help clients find a career path 

Eligibility guidelines and CRITERIA for acceptance

Candidates for our program are:
- Capable of attending minimum of 2 sessions per week
- 15 - 25 years old
- Able to read and write at the 5th grade level

Do any of the following apply to you?

Have you committed a juvenile offense?

Are you currently enrolled in a substance abuse-reduction or relapse-prevention program?

Are you seeking skills for employment?

Are you formerly incarcerated and looking for life skills and re-integration?
